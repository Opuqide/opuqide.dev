Introduction

For those new to Apple-internal stuff, meet the one important component of the iOS and macOS operating systems: iBoot. Yes, that's right. iBoot is the stage-2 bootloader flashed onto iPhones, iPads, MacBooks, eh, you name it! But, what Apple didn't tell you fully in their Platform Security Guide was how iOS booted into an operating system.

Apple keeps their proprietary software closed source, and so is iBoot. But, with the help of the source code from 7 years ago, we can take a look at how iBoot gets into an OS.
